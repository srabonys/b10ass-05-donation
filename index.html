Aid for Injured in the Quota Movement

The Quota Movement in Bangladesh, which sought to reform the quota system for public sector jobs, led to significant protests where many demonstrators were injured. In response, various organizations and government agencies mobilized to provide aid for those affected. This assistance included medical treatment for injuries, financial support for rehabilitation, and psychological counseling to help victims cope with the trauma of the events. Community support also played a crucial role, with local groups organizing fundraisers and awareness campaigns to assist the injured and advocate for their rights, highlighting the ongoing struggle for equitable job opportunities.
